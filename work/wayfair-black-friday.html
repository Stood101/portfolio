The Aim of this research is to know if customer scorces impact the precentage discounts for each products from WayFairs' datasets of Black Friday deals. Analysing the promotion strategy and gathering insights to cater the future strategy on specific products.

Specifically, the Purpose of the case is to answer the question, “Do customer review scores affect percentage discounts during the Black Friday sale?”

The scatter plot shows a slight upward trend in the blue regression line, indicating a weak positive linear relationship between the average rating and percentage discount. As average ratings increase, discounts tend to increase marginally. Additonally, there are insights as -

Data points are widely scattered around the regression line, showing a high variability in percentage discounts across all rating levels which also has deviations of 20.5 percentage. Products with similar average ratings (e.g., around 4.5) display discounts ranging from as low as 10% to as high as 80%. This wide dispersion suggests that discount strategies are influenced by multiple factors and are not strongly dependent on ratings.

A portion of the data points is concentrated around higher average ratings between 4.5 - 5.0. Despite the generally high ratings, there is considerable variation in discounts raning from minimum of 10 to heavy discounts reaching 82. This indicates that even high products are subjected to significant discounting possibly due to factors other than ratings (such as inventory clearance or efforts to maximize sales volume).

We can also observe few outliers in the graph where products receive very high discounts of 70–80 irrespective of their average ratings. These outliers occur across multiple rating levels, from as low as 3.5 to as high as 5.0. This depictes certain discounts are applied independently of product reviews and are likely driven by external factors (such as inventory management or campaigns).

SELECT 
    pd.product_id,
    r.review_id,
    (5 * r.five_star + 4 * r.four_star + 3 * r.three_star + 2 * r.two_star + 1 * r.one_star) / 
    NULLIF(r.five_star + r.four_star + r.three_star + r.two_star + r.one_star, 0) AS average_rating,
    pr.original_price,
    pr.discounted_price,
    ##Calculating discount percentage
    ROUND(((pr.discounted_price/ pr.original_price)-1) *-100,2) AS discount_percentage,
    #pr.black_friday_deal,
    cat.category_id
FROM 
    Product AS pd

#Joins with aliasing
JOIN 
    Review AS r ON pd.review_id = r.review_id
JOIN 
    Price AS pr ON pd.price_id = pr.price_id
JOIN 
	ProCat AS pc ON pd.product_id = pc.product_id
JOIN 
	Category As cat ON pc.category_id = cat.category_id
WHERE 
    pr.black_friday_deal = 1;

#Creating a scatterplot with a line of best fit.

x = WayFair['average_rating']        ## Independent variable
y = WayFair['discount_percentage']   ## Dependent variable

plt.scatter(x, y, color = 'orange', label=  'Products data points')

#For the line of best fit
coefficients = np.polyfit(x, y, 1)  
line = np.poly1d(coefficients)

x_line = np.linspace(min(x), max(x), 100)
y_line = line(x_line)

plt.plot(x_line, y_line, color = 'blue', label = f'Relation line')

plt.xlabel('Average Rating')
plt.ylabel('Percentage Discount')
plt.title('Is there a linear relationship between discounts and reviews?')
plt.legend()

plt.show()

The scatter plot demonstrates the line of best fit shows there no association betwwen discount percentages and reviews altough they go in the same direction.
