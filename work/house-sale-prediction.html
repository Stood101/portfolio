Model defending: We have developed a strong prediction model because of the following:

  1. Simple transformation and missing values:

In order to have a good predictive model, we analyzed the missing values and saw that most of them were empty because the houses did not had that feature. So instead of dropping them, we fill them with zeros or median to have a better understanding of what may give more or less value to the sale price. Then, we transformed all the categorical features into dummies because we saw that it was easier when deciding what we thought was important to have in a house as a group, therefore it could help create new features as well.

  1. Creative features:

Instead of only using the given data to predict sale prices, we created new variables that allowed us to make key decisions on what we want for our "ideal house". We have 6 features that can help any buyer make quick decisions without having to look for much detail or use formulas:

  • Garage score: We wanted to know if a garage has the minimum quality and condition needed, therefore we created a score from 0 to 6 where the closer it is to 6 means it is a built in garage (way more convenient for security and storage purposes) in good conditions and has enough space in comparison to the total square feet of the house.
  • House and Garage age: We wanted to give the buyer visibility on how old both the garage and the house are by looking at the word instead of making a subtraction with the year the house was built.

-Ideal location: Every buyer wants to know if a house is close or not to parks, schools and hospitals, so instead of having to search for the neighborhood on Google, we decided to create a feature that selects the best neighborhoods with the proximity locations. -Basement Score: We wanted to know if a basement is good enough to meet all our needs, that is why we created a score based on the quality and condition, as well as the exposure it has so that the buyer can quickly asses if it is ideal or not. -Total bathrooms: We wanted to provide the total number of bathrooms in the house because the data had them all spread out in total, half and basement bathrooms.

3.Application of domain knowledge: This model helps in selecting the best features for predicting house prices by analyzing their correlation with the target variable. It also creates new features that have a strong relationship with sale price, focusing on factors that truly influence home-buying decisions. Visualizations play a key role in identifying trends, making it easier to understand how different aspects impact pricing. To handle categorical data effectively, all categorical columns are encoded as 1s and 0s, ensuring they can be used in numerical modeling. Additionally, the model applies stepwise selection with Ordinary Least Squares (OLS) regression, adding important features and removing less useful ones based on statistical significance. This approach helps cut out noise, avoid redundancy, and improve accuracy. By combining correlation analysis, trend visualization, categorical encoding, and linear regression, the model provides a clear and data-driven way to predict house prices effectively.

Feature Engineering

Feature 1: Garage score

This garage score will help us quickly discern which garages are Built In, Finished, and in Good or Average condition. We decided that if a garage is Built In it will be better for safety purposes, such as not having to worry about broken windows, leaving the car door open or parking tickets. We also wanted a Finished garage because we don't want to worry about spending money on construction, but since it is not that important we used a weight of 0.5. Moreover, the garage has to be above average quality and condition, good enough to be used without having to spend more money on renovations that could lead into not being able to use the garage. Finally, the comfort aspect of not having to look for a parking space every day or walk with grocery bags from the parking space to your house is a big plus for our ideal house.

Our hypothesis is that the garage area per car ratio will have a strong positive correlation with sale price because bigger garages increase the price of the house, particularly in areas where commuting in public transport takes too much time. Also, since public parking spaces are reducing every year and the price of parking per hour is increasing, garages become an asset that will never lose its value even when you don't have a car, it can be rented and used as an income, or as an storage space.

When looking at the results, we see that most of the scores are higher than 2, meaning that more than half of the houses have an ideal garage.

Feature 5: Basement Score

Our feature gives a score based on the total finished space that is allocated to the basement. Our hypothesis is that the score will have a strong positive correlation with sale price. In regions with extreme seasonal temperatures (below 0°C in winter and above 30°C in summer), basements provide a secure space for essential heating and cooling systems, protecting them from damage and ensuring their proper function, which increases property value. Additionally, basements act as an extra floor with fewer usage restrictions, enhancing the home's desirability and market price. b) A good size for a basement should be able to give us an easier access to plumbing, ducts and electrical panel, and enough extra space to storage things or use it as a quiet space for an office, gym or entertainment area. Since the average ratio needed for all the mechanical equipment in a 1500 SF house is around 0.3, a score higher than that value will be considered as a basement that does not fit all the things / conditions we desire, higher than that means there is enough space for any mechanical equipment, and you can also have extra storage space, an office or a gym with the benefit of having a above average quality, condition and exposure.
